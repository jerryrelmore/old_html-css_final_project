A little bit of my background: I grew up mostly outside the small town (pop. ~500) of Argonia, KS, though we moved around a lot when I was young. After my junior year of high school, I finished school in Wichita, worked for a couple of years, then took off for the Army. I was lucky enough to travel around the world more than once before returning to Kansas. Except for a two-year stint in western Kentucky, I've pretty much lived in Wichita since I got out in 2004. I'm currently a program manager for a semiconductor test lab here in Wichita. While the daily details of my job are pretty boring, we do some neat work. Some of the parts we tested are on Mars while others are headed out of the Solar System.

I'm a big fan of furthering my education - so much so that I've been in college (more on than off) since 1998. I have bachelor and master's degrees in anthropology and in a former life I was an archaeologist. Currently working on an associate's in software development or pre-engineering (depending upon the semester) at Butler and will return to WSU sometime in the next couple of years.
